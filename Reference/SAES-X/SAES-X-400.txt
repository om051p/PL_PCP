                  Scope
                 This standard prescribes the minimum mandatory requirements governing the design and
                 installation of cathodic protection systems for onshore pressurized buried metallic pipelines
                 located outside of plant facilities. Typical pipelines addressed by this standard are; cross-
                 country, production, and utility pipelines.
                 Subsea pipelines and pipelines operated by Saudi Aramco Community Maintenance
                 Departments are outside the scope of this standard (refer to SAES-X-300 for cathodic protection
                 of marine structures).
                  Conflicts and Deviations
                  Any conflicts between this document and other applicable Mandatory Saudi Aramco
                  Engineering Requirements (MSAERs) shall be addressed to the EK&RD Manager.
                  Any deviation from the requirements herein shall follow internal company procedure SAEP-302.

                 Definitions
                 The following general list of definitions and abbreviations has been developed specifically for
                 application with Saudi Aramco cathodic protection standards and specifications:
                  AA:                          The ANSI cooling class for a dry-type self-cooled transformer or
                                               reactor that is cooled by the natural circulation of air. Syn.: AN (IEC),
                                               see definition 3.1 in IEEE C57.12.
                  AC:                          Alternating current.
                  AC Coupon:                   An alternating current coupon, with a surface area of 1 cm2, used to
                                               measure representative AC potentials or AC current densities on a
                                               pipeline or other buried or immersed metallic structure.
                  ACM:                         Alternating Current Mitigation is the use of a design and construction
                                               methodology that specifically reduces the otherwise unsafe AC step
                                               and touch potentials on a pipeline to within recognized safe limits.
                                               The effects of fault current events and the associated Ground
                                               Potential Rise concerns are considered in addition to induced AC
                                               voltages arising from Normal and Emergency steady state operating
                                               conditions of High Voltage Overhead Powerlines.
                  Anode:                       An impressed current or a galvanic anode for cathodic protection
                                               applications.
                  Anode Cable:                 A cable directly connected to an impressed current or galvanic anode.

                  ANV:
                                               The ANSI cooling class for a dry-type non-ventilated self-cooled
                                               transformer, which is so constructed as to provide no intentional
                                               circulation of external air through the transformer, and that operates
                                               at zero gauge pressure. Syn.: ANAN (IEC), see definition 3.15 in IEEE
                                               C57.12.
                  Armored Cable:
                                               Armored cable for cathodic protection is a single core insulated cable
                                               manufactured with a double layer spiral wound insulated galvanized
                                               steel sheath to provide mechanical protection, and typically used for
                                               subsea applications.
                  Bond Cable:
                                               A cable installed between two metallic structures to provide electrical
                                               continuity between the structures for the purpose of cathodic
                                               protection.
                  Buyer:
                                               Saudi Aramco Purchasing Department Representative.
                  Buyer's
                  Representative:              The person or persons designated by the Purchasing Department to
                                               monitor/enforce the contract. Normally, this is the on-site inspector.
                  Calcined Petroleum
                  Coke Breeze:                 A carbonaceous backfill used as a conductive backfill media for
                                               impressed current anodes in soil.
                  Christmas Tree:
                                               The set of valves, spools and fittings connected to the top of a well to
                                               direct and control the flow of formation fluids from the well.
                  Coated Casing:
                                               The term “coated casing” as used in this engineering standard
                                               describes a well casing with an external non-conductive coating
                                               (typically Fusion Bonded Epoxy or FBE). The coating must be applied
                                               to all sections of the casing in contact with soil or formation, from
                                               surface to the bottom of the casing or to a depth determined to
                                               facilitate external corrosion mitigation with cathodic protection
                                               through the relevant down hole corrosive aquifers. Casings that have
                                               been coated over the upper two or three joints of casing only are not
                                               “coated casings”. Coating applied to well casings is not applied as a
                                               corrosion barrier. It is applied to reduce the total amount of CP
                                               required or to extend the influence of the applied CP.
                  CP Assessment
                  Probe:                       A multi-electrode probe custom fabricated for Saudi Aramco that is
                                               designed to facilitate representative measurements of instant off,
                                               total polarization, current density, and resistivity.
                  CP Coupon:
                                               A cathodic protection coupon used to measure representative
                                               potentials or current densities on a pipeline or other buried or
                                               immersed metallic structure.

                  Circuit Resistance:          The total resistance seen at the output of a CP power supply or the

                  CP System Operating
                  Circuit Resistance:          The total resistance seen at the output of a CP power supply or the
                                               total working resistance in a galvanic anode system.
                  CP System Rated
                  Circuit Resistance:          The rated output voltage of a cathodic protection power supply
                                               divided by the rated output current. For photovoltaic power supplies,
                                               the rated output current is the design commissioning current.
                  Cross Country
                  Pipeline:                    A pipeline between; two plant areas, another cross country pipeline
                                               and a plant area, or between two cross country pipelines.
                  CSE:
                                               Saturated copper/copper sulfate reference electrode.
                  CSV:
                                               Coating Stress Voltage. This is the voltage that a pipeline coating will
                                               be subjected to in the event of a fault current event arising from a
                                               nearby High Voltage Overhead Powerline that may cause coating
                                               failure or significant degradation should the dielectric strength of the
                                               coating not be high enough to withstand the stress voltage.
                  Deep Anode Bed:
                                               Anode or anodes connected to a common CP power supply and
                                               installed in a vertical hole with a depth exceeding 15 m (50 ft).
                  DCD:
                                               Direct Current Decoupling Device. This is a device that is installed
                                               between the pipeline or structure that may be subject to AC
                                               interference with the risk or arcing or high AC potentials developing.
                                               These devices may eliminate or greatly reduce the induced potentials
                                               resulting during normal operation or surge conditions and reduce the
                                               possibility of arcing and structure damage.
                  Design Agency:
                                               The organization responsible for the design of the pipeline and
                                               associated CP system. The Design Agency may be the Design
                                               Contractor, the Lump Sum Turn Key Contractor or an in house design
                                               organization of Saudi Aramco.
                  Drain Point:
                                               The location on the cathodically protected structure where the
                                               negative cable from the rectifier or negative junction box is fastened
                                               to the structure.
                  EMF:
                                               Electromagnetic field. Magnetic field related to AC interference
                                               arising from high voltage AC overhead powerlines.
                  ERP:
                                               Electrical Resistance Probes and instruments determine metal loss
                                               from corrosion or erosion by the electrical resistance method.

                 Fundamental Design Calculations

                  consideration which shall be clearly identified in the project proposal.
                  -    the new pipeline and associated CP equipment to be an independent system
                  -    the new pipeline as a part of the existing pipeline corridor which considers spare capacity of
                       existing systems, existing pipeline protection status and upgradation of existing system
                  The Project Proposal shall identify all locations where the new pipeline will be mechanically
                  connected to existing facilities with or without electrical isolation and additional cathodic
                  protection requirements shall be assessed accordingly.

                  Fundamental design calculations shall include:
                      a. Determination of the total current required.
                      b. Optimum placement and sizing of the anode beds and CP power supplies with
                         consideration given to maintaining potentials on the pipeline within the criteria detailed
                         in Table 4.
                      c. For calculations, assume the following:
                      •   A native state potential of -600 millivolts (CSE), and
                      •   A pipeline coating conductivity for as listed below should be used for design calculations
                          for new coated pipelines unless the actual coating conductivity measurements values
                          and/or site conditions negate the use of these values.

                  Permanent ICCP systems operating at 75% of rated current output.

                  Minimum and maximum instant off Potential values of 850 and 1,150 (-mV) Permanent galvanic
                  anodes installed on the pipeline at locations such as road crossings or thrust anchors. For these

                 Temporary Cathodic Protection
   6.7.1          Temporary CP shall be installed to provide short term cathodic protection only when:
                      a. The permanent ICCP will not be energized within 30 days of burial of any portion of the
                         pipeline, and
                      b. The magnesium anodes and cross bonds installed during pipeline construction for
                         permanent cathodic protection requirements will not provide enough CP (to achieve
                         temporary criteria) for the new pipeline or pipeline segment.
                          Note:
                          The current density requirements and protection levels required for temporary CP can be found in
                          Table 4 of this standard.
   6.7.2          The design life of temporary cathodic protection shall be 2 years minimum. However, if for
                  some reason at the end of 2-year period the permanent CP system cannot be installed and/or
                  commissioned, life of temporary CP system may be extended after obtaining a specific return
                  approval for such life extension from Proponent. Design calculations for additional temporary
                  anodes along with pipeline protection status for 2-year period shall be submitted to Saudi
                  Aramco for review and approval.
   6.7.3          The design for temporary cathodic protection shall consider the effect of the CP current that will
                  be provided through the bonds that are going to be installed to parallel and crossing pipelines,
                  and galvanic anodes installed at road crossing and thrust anchors.
                  Whilst bonding of all the pipelines in the corridor is a requirement (see Section 6.10), provision
                  for bonding in the form of pipe to cable connection and cable termination at the bond box shall
                  be considered in the design and installation. However, decision to actually carryout bonding or
                  when to bond is to be made by Proponent taking into consideration the protection status of
                  existing pipeline, impact of bonding to the new and the old pipelines, etc.
   6.7.4          Where supplemental galvanic anodes are determined to be necessary for temporary cathodic
                  protection, wherever practical, they shall be installed at permanent galvanic anode locations.
                  The permanent junction box or test station facilities shall be used to terminate the supplemental
                  galvanic anodes.
   6.7.5          Pipe-to soil potential survey data verifying that the temporary system is providing adequate
                  protection shall be provided by PMT and submitted to the CP proponent monthly for the first 6
                  months, and every 6 months thereafter till the commissioning of the impressed current system.
   6.7.6          At locations where the permanent galvanic anode equipment will not work for effective
                  temporary cathodic protection:
                      •   Select low resistivity locations to maximize the current output and minimize the number
                          of anodes required.
                      •   Install galvanic anodes on an “as required” basis, i.e., use the calculated numbers of
                          anodes as a guide, and install anodes as required based on meeting the temporary CP
                          protection criteria (Table 4).
   6.7.7          Terminate galvanic anodes for temporary cathodic protection in a 3-pin test station using the
                  installation details given in Standard Drawing AA-036352. The number of anodes required per
                  location shall be based on the actual requirement and not as shown on the standard drawing.
                  If the location for the 3-pin test station location coincides with a KM marker/CP test station
                  location, a 1-pin test station is not required.

                 Cased Pipelines
                  Casing may affect effectiveness of cathodic protection on the carrier pipelines and hence not
                  desirable. However, various types of casing may be used as in some situations as

                  When coated metallic casings or coated concrete casings are used, as these shield cathodic
                  protection of the carrier pipe, it must ensure that carrier pipe is well coated and there should no
                  damage to coating of the carrier pipe inside the casing during installation. Proper electrical
                  isolation of the carrier pipe and casing pipe must be maintained at all times. Further to protect
                  the carrier pipe inside the casing any one or combination of following may be used.

                       A-134),
                  -    If filling of the annulus is not feasible, zinc ribbon anodes should be provided inside the
                       annulus to protect the carrier pipe and connected to the carrier pipe through a test station.
                  When uncoated or bare metallic casings are used, the pipeline shall be electrically insulated
                  from the casing using non-metallic spacers. The casing should be sealed using non-metallic
                  end seals and provided with vent pipes at both ends.

                 Stray Current Corrosion
                  Stray currents include DC or AC. DC stray currents are from neighboring Cathodic Protection
                  system, DC traction systems, welding operations, etc.AC stray current is usually as a result of
                  Induced AC voltages on the pipeline as a result its proximity to the high voltage power lines.
   6.14.1         DC Stray Current Corrosion
                  DC induced stray current is usually indicated by localized fluctuations to the cathodic protection
                  potentials which could be static or dynamic depending on the source of stray current.
                  DC stray current can be mitigated by one or several of the following methods:
                  -    Installation of a bonding / drainage station between the two pipeline structures;
                  -    Installation of isolating joint;
                  -    By adjusting the cathodic protection system settings to improve the cathodic protection
                       levels; and
                  -    Localized cathodic protection systems.
                  Refer to NACE SP0169, Control of External Corrosion on Underground or Submerged Metallic
                  Piping Systems as well as Annexure-B of ISO 15589-1, Petroleum, Petrochemical, and Natural
                  Gas Industries - Cathodic Protection of Pipeline Systems On-land Pipelines.
                  When DC stray current interference are expected or found, mitigation design and materials
                  requirements shall be submitted to Saudi Aramco for review and approval.
                  At the time of commissioning of CP system stray current interference tests shall be carried out
                  at pipeline crossings and parallelism locations to confirm that there is no stray current
                  interference. Stray current interference if any found shall be mitigated. These field testing &
                  mitigation for stray current shall be submitted to proponent for acceptance.


                 AC Interference and Mitigation
                  AC interference on a buried pipeline can cause corrosion due to AC current exchange between
                  the pipeline and and the surrounding soil. This exchange current depends on several factors
                  listed below.
                  -    AC voltage
                  -    Configuration of the AC power line phase conductors
                  -    Configuration of the earthing conductors
                  -    Distance between the AC power line and the pipeline
                  -    Current flowing the AC power line phase conductors
                  -    Average coating resistance of the pipeline

   6.15.5         AC Corrosion
                  From the AC voltages measured on the pipeline, AC current density shall be estimated utilizing
                  below formula.
                                             iac = 8 Vac / ρπd
                              where
                                     iac – Calculated AC Current density
                                     Vac – Measured AC voltage on the pipeline near the powerline
                                     ρ – Soil Resistivity around the pipeline where the AC voltages were measured
                                     d – Holiday diameter (use standard value of 1.12 cm unless experience indicates
                                          otherwise)
                              From the above calculations, if iac is found to be higher than 20 amps/m2, then suitable
                              mitigation measures must be carried out to mitigate AC potential and AC corrosion by
                              one or combination of below recommendation.
                              - Zinc grounding wires/electrodes
                              - Copper grounding with DC isolation
                              - Polarization Cell / DC decoupling devices
                              - Gradient control mats
                              Refer to NACE standard - SP0177 - Mitigation of Alternating Current and Lightning
                              Effects on Metallic Structures and Corrosion Control.

                 Circuit Resistance Criteria
   7.5.1          The “rated” circuit resistance for an impressed current CP power supply shall be defined as the
                  power supply rated dc voltage divided by the rated dc current. Rated voltages and currents are
                  as detailed on the manufacturer's data sheet/plate.
   7.5.2          The “operating” circuit resistance for CP system shall be defined as the total effective resistance
                  seen by the output terminals of the respective rectifier or photovoltaic output control center
                  (OCC).
   7.5.3          For a galvanic CP system, the “operating” circuit resistance shall be defined as the open circuit
                  voltage difference between the anodes and the protected pipeline.
   7.5.4          For calculation purposes, the operating circuit resistance shall include:
                      a. Anode bed resistance to ground.
                      b. Positive cable resistance from CP power source to anodes.
                      c. Negative cable resistance from CP power source to structure.
                      d. Resistance of the pipeline to remote earth (for calculation purposes shall be 0.055 ohms
                         unless site testing is completed to verify a more accurate value).
                      e. Effective resistance caused by 0.8 volts anode bed back emf (ICCP only) plus the 1.2 volt
                         structure back emf (example: 1.2 volts pipeline + 0.8 volts ICCP anode bed back emf =
                         2.0 volts total between the anode with coke breeze backfill, and a coated pipeline).
                                 Remf       =       (0.8v + 1.2v) / Irated
                                 Irated     =       CP power supply “rated” current
                    Exception:
                    CP power supplies with a rated voltage of 12 volts or less shall consider the total back emf to be 1.2 volts
                    for calculation purposes.

                    Note:
                    When using Remf to calculate the maximum allowable anode bed resistance to provide 70% of
                    Rrated, the value of Remf must be multiplied by 0.7.
                                    Example: Ranodebed maximum = (0.7 x (Rrated - Remf)) - Rcables - Rstructure.
   7.5.5          New CP systems shall be designed to achieve an “operating” circuit resistance less than or equal
                  to 70% of the CP power supply “rated” circuit resistance.
                                          For design:             Rop ≤ 0.7 x Rrated
                      Where:
                                          Rop = CP system “operating” circuit resistance
                                          Rrated = CP power supply “rated” circuit resistance
