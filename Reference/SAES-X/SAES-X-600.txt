                 Scope
                 This standard prescribes the minimum mandatory requirements governing the design and
                 installation of cathodic protection systems for the external surfaces of buried and submerged
                 structures for onshore plant facilities. This standard does not address buried pipelines entering
                 or exiting the plant before the launcher/receiver area.
                 Plant facilities that require cathodic protection include but are not limited to the following
                 structures:
                      Buried pressurized piping including short sections at road crossings, dike crossings, or at
                      connections to existing underground piping.
                      Buried non-pressurized piping containing a product that might pose a safety or
                      environmental concern if leakage was to occur, i.e., gravity drain lines with H2S.
                      Only if economically viable and when stray current corrosion could be a concern, non-
                      pressurized buried hydrocarbon piping shall be electrically bonded to the CP systems, but
                      does not need to comply with any protection criteria.
                      Vent pipes and non-pressurized instrument air pipes do not require cathodic protection.
                      Pipeline casings for pressurized piping at road or dike crossings.
                      The bottom or soil-side of above ground storage tanks.
                      Buried metallic storage tanks.
                      Critical metal piles, onshore side of sheet piling and associated anchors. Non-critical metal
                      piles shall be coated for the entire length with APCS-113A as per SAES-H-002 and do not
                      require cathodic protection.
                      Buried valves and fittings connected to non-metallic underground piping.
                      Water wells inside the plant facilities shall be protected as detailed in SAES-X-700.
                      Catch basins and metal fittings and appurtenances associated with catch basins are outside
                      the scope of this standard.
                 This standard may not be attached to nor made part of purchase orders.

   5.1.1         Contractor/Designer Qualifications
                 Cathodic protection designs shall be carried out by design offices that are approved by Saudi
                 Aramco. Such design offices must have qualified engineers with a minimum of five years
                 verifiable cathodic protection design experience and a minimum industry qualification of NACE
                 CP Level 4 and BS degree. NACE CP certification level, certification number, name of designer,
                 and his signature/initial shall be provided in the design package.
                         Note:
                         Review, validation and approval of 90% detailed design package is responsibility of CSD and it is
                         not delegated to anyone (including the approved designers).
                 Field measurements required for the design shall be collected by an Engineer or Technician with
                 a minimum of five years verifiable cathodic protection field experience and industry certification
                 level of NACE CP Level 2 or higher. Installation supervision of cathodic protection materials
                 shall be done by Engineer or Technician with a minimum of five years verifiable cathodic
                 protection field experience and industry certification level of NACE CP Level 2 or higher.
                         Note:
                         PMT may request assistance from CSD for the verification of the qualifications of the Design
                         Contractor’s engineer/technicians responsible for the CP field and engineering work.
   5.1.2         Design Review
                 Proposed construction drawings and related cathodic protection design information in
                 accordance with SAEP-303 shall be submitted to the Proponent cathodic protection
                 organization and to Saudi Aramco’s Consulting Services Department (CSD) for review and
                 approval.
                 The design agency shall not issue drawings for construction until the design has been reviewed
                 and approved in writing by CSD and the Proponent cathodic protection organization.
                         Exception:
                         Design package approval is not required for CP designs based specifically on a CP standard
                         drawing, e.g., galvanic anode installations per Standard Drawing AA-036352, for road crossings on
                         normally above grade pipelines with no dedicated CP systems.
                 The design package submitted for review shall contain at minimum:
                     The scope of work
                     Professionally drafted full size Index “X” CP drawings that:
                 • Detail each CP item by description and stock number if applicable.
                 • Detail the proposed location for each piece of CP equipment including but not limited to
                     rectifiers, anodes, junction boxes, bond stations, test stations, soil access holes, bonds to
                     pipelines, insulating flanges and unions.
                 • Detail and clearly illustrate an organized numbering system (sequential order) for test
                     points and soil access boxes that begins at the top left corner of the cathodic protection
                     layout drawing.
                 • Detail and specifically identify all cathodic protection cables including all anode, structure,
                     bond, and rectifier cables. These details shall be shown on the One-Line Diagram for
                     Impressed Current Cathodic Protection (ICCP) system.
                 • Clearly identify the specific and individual cable routing and termination points within the
                     respective test stations, junction boxes, bond boxes, and rectifiers.
                 • Detail all cathodic protection equipment using the cathodic protection symbols as shown
                     on Standard Drawing AD-036785 “Symbols for Cathodic Protection.”
                 • Detail all existing CP installations in the plant that could have an effect on the new CP
                     system, and also provide information on spare current capacity of existing CP systems that
                     will be utilized by the project.

                                   comprehensive cathodic protection system design including all required field
                                   measurement data, calculations, a detailed description of the proposed cathodic
                                   protection equipment, and a set of detailed drawings that illustrate the proposed
                                   placement of all cathodic protection equipment and the associated protected structures.

   5.2.1         Design Philosophy
                 Unless otherwise specified in this standard, cathodic protection systems shall be designed using
                 the “earth potential rise” method with distributed, continuous linear anode or with MMO grid
                 type impressed current anodes.
                           Exception:

                           be more cost effective in some situations. Proposals must be submitted with a complete and
                           thorough economic comparison and shall be considered unacceptable unless pre-approved in
                           writing by the proponent.

                           drawings.

   5.2.5         Circuit Resistance
                 For a T/R, the CP system rated circuit resistance shall be defined as the T/R rated voltage,
                 divided by the T/R rated current. Rated voltages and currents are as detailed on the
                 manufacturer's data sheet/plate.
                 The CP system operating circuit resistance for an ICCP system shall be defined as the total
                 effective resistance as seen by the output terminals of the respective ICCP power supply, and for
                 calculation purposes shall include:
                      Anode bed resistance to ground.
                      Positive cable resistance from CP power source to anodes.
                      Negative cable resistance from CP power source to structure.
                      Resistance of the structure to remote earth.
                      Unless otherwise stated, use a value of 0.01 ohm.
                      Effective resistance caused by +0.8 volts anode bed back emf (for HSCI in coke breeze) plus
                      the structure back emf of -1.2 volts = 2.0 volts total between the anode with coke breeze
                      backfill, and a coated buried piping.
                      Remf=(0.8 v + back emf) / Irated
                      Irated=CP power supply “rated” current.
                         Exception:
                         A back emf of +1.0 volts (Cu/CuSO4) shall be used for mixed metal oxide anodes in water tanks,
                         subkha, or comparable high salinity wet applications without coke breeze. This does not include the
                         back emf associated with the polarized potential of the structure.
                         Note:
                         When using Remf to calculate the maximum allowable anode bed resistance to provide 70% of
                         Rrated, the value of Remf must be multiplied by 0.7.
                         Example: Ranodebed maximum = (0.7 x (Rrated - Remf)) - Rcables - Rstructure.

   5.3.4         Anode Beds
                 The number and size requirements for the anodes shall be determined based on providing a
                 sufficient potential gradient over the entire length of the respective pipeline to achieve the
                 protection criteria.

                 resistance of the rectifier, however, the combined current capacity of the anodes shall be equal
                 to or greater than the rectifier rated current.
                 Anode spacing shall be considered based on the voltage gradient calculations. It is required to
                 ensure that the recommended anode quantity and spacing covers the entire piping network
                 that is part of the CP system. One good approach to satisfy this requirement is to verify on the
                 plot plan with known area, anodes are spaced enough based on voltage gradient and then draw
                 a circle of gradient for each anode to show that these gradient circles overlap each and other
                 and covers the entire piping under consideration. ‘A0’ sized plot plan drawing showing anode
                 layout, anode voltage gradient circles (anode voltage cones) and UG piping shall be provided
                 with the design for review.
                 For plant plot that exceed 50 m by 50 m, soil resistivity survey at multiple locations shall be
                 carried out and the worst case values (lowest resistivity values) used in the anode voltage
                 gradient calculations.
                 If impressed current anodes are placed within 50 meters of a thrust anchor, then two additional
                 impressed current anodes shall be installed within 15 meters of the anchor, and placed on
                 opposite sides of the anchor. If impressed current anodes are installed, galvanic anodes are not
                 required for the thrust anchor.

                         Cathodic protection requirements for thrust anchors on pipelines exiting or entering the plant are
                         covered in SAES-X-400.

                 piping; provided the following conditions are met:
                      All parts of the piping are greater than 100 meters from the closest impressed current
                      rectifier.

                      wood, and metal) are within 6 meters of the piping.

   5.3.7         Buried Metal Pipelines - Temporary Cathodic Protection
                 Temporary CP of plant piping sometimes impractical due to arrangement and construction;
                 therefore, requirement for temporary CP shall be carefully evaluated at the project design stage.
                 Various factors that needs to be considered for such evaluations include but not limited to
                 following:
                 • Soil corrosivity
                 • Presence of copper earthing network
                 • Other corrosion protection measures such as coating, corrosion allowance considerations
                 • construction period
                 If the permanent ICCP will not be energized within 6 months of burial of any portion of the
                 pipeline, then temporary CP shall be provided.
                 The current density requirements for temporary CP shall be 10% of current density stated in
                 Table 2 of this standard.
                 The design life of temporary cathodic protection shall be 2 years minimum.
                 Instead of separate temporary CP system, permanent CP installations can be temporarily
                 energized to achieve temporary protection.
                 Where supplemental galvanic anodes are determined to be necessary for temporary cathodic
                 protection, wherever practical, they shall be installed at permanent galvanic anode locations.

   5.3.8         Cathodic Protection of existing unprotected Pipelines
                 When it is desired to cathodically protect existing unprotected structures, considering that these
                 are existing pipelines whose condition is unknown, it is recommended to carry out a current
                 injection test. Current injection test in addition to providing current requirement for CP, also
                 verifies the capability of cathodic protection system to protect the structure of interest. Such
                 current injection tests can be carried out utilizing temporary DC power sources (including
                 batteries with variable resistors), temporary anode beds (usually made of scrap steel) to
                 determine current required for cathodic protection and probable locations for anode beds.
                 For existing structures prior to design of CP system, other asset integrity tests such as physical
                 condition assessment (sample dig survey), fitness for purpose, leak and/or failure history, etc…,
                 shall be assessed. When cathodically protecting existing unprotected structures, it shall be
                 brought to the notice of all concerned including the proponent that effective CP system whilst
                 providing protection from further corrosion does not correct any pre-existing corrosion which
                 may still result in accelerated corrosion and/or failure and hence over reliance on CP under such
                 conditions shall be avoided unless all such pre-existing corrosion defects have been identified
                 and repaired.

   5.5.1         New Tanks - CP Design Philosophy
                 The cathodic protection system shall be designed in accordance with the requirements for new

                         In case of a tank(s) containing high temperature product(s) typically in the region of 90°C and
                         above that could make the soil environment under the tank bottom to be very dry making the CP
                         system ineffective, then there is no need for a CP system to protect the tank(s) bottom. However,
                         this commentary note is only applicable when the said tanks are operating at high temperatures. If

                         potentially be installed.

                         - Tank operating temperature envelope
                         - Coating types (internal and external)
                         - Thicknesses and coating heat transfer values

                 It is up to the proponent organization to evaluate the corrosion history of the tank(s) and to

                 If the decision is to install a cathodic protection system, then the following procedure shall be
                 implemented:
                     Conduct soil resistivity measurements approximately 10 meters from the tank to determine
                     uniformity of the soil surrounding the tank and to determine the optimum depth and
                     orientation to place the anodes.

                     place from the tank.
                     Design and install the anodes circumferentially distributed around the tank based on the
                     information gathered from the above testing. Conduct this field testing for each group of
                     tanks within the same plant sharing similar soil characteristics.

                 For new tank bottom CP system installation, the negative structure-to-soil potential shall be
                 measured at 1 meter intervals through a slotted monitoring tube installed beneath the tank,
                 and through access holes in the ring wall. The protection criteria for tank bottoms and buried
                 tanks shall be:
                     A minimum of -1.0 volt and a maximum of -2.5 volts, with reference to a copper/copper
                     sulfate electrode and the current on, or
                     if all of the protective current can be interrupted, a minimum potential of either -0.850 volt
                     off with reference to a copper/copper sulfate electrode, or
                     a 100 mV polarization shift between the instant off potential and either the native state (for
                     initial commissioning only), or the depolarized state potential.
                         Notes:

                                      with all influencing CP power supplies turned ON and if with the help of a coupon or
                                      other reliable method if it can be demonstrated that the instant OFF values are within the
                                      acceptable limits and in such specific cases, higher ON potentials than the limit stated will
                                      be allowed.
                                 2.   Criteria c. (Polarization Potential) The “potential decay” is the decrease in potential
                                      between the “instant off” and the steady state off potential. A probe or coupon typically
                                      reaches a steady state off potential within 60 seconds to 60 minutes. Refer to ISO15589-1
                                      and NACE TM0497 for depolarization measurement procedures. This criteria shall not be
                                      used when mixed-metal components or dissimilar metals are in contact for example steel
                                      pipeline in contact with copper (pipe or earthing). And in soils containing Sulfate

             Appendix B – Tank Bottom CP Testing Procedure for Tanks Sitting on Oily-Sand Pads


          Trip #1:
          Objective:
          Conduct soil resistivity measurement to specify optimum remote anode depth that will
               generate the highest voltage gradient.
          CONTRACTOR will:
          1.      Pick a (remote) location around 10-40 meters away (10 meters for low resistivity areas
                  and 40 meters for high resistivity areas) from existing tank under T&I specified by
                  Proponent, free from any buried metallic structures.
          2.      Measure remote 4-pin soil resistivity at the proposed remote anode location at depths
                  from 1 to 6 m with 0.5 m pin-spacing intervals, in low resistivity areas conducive to
                  four pin measurements. In high resistivity areas, four pin measurements and Geonics

                  the Proponent to install one remote anode.

          Trip # 3:
          Objective:
          Determine:
          1)      coupon corrosion potential.
          2)      coupon-to-coupon potential difference.
          3)      coupon-to-ground contact resistance.
          4)      coupon current pick up.
          5)      100-mV polarization shift (on coupon).
          6)      voltage gradient mapping outside the tank going away from each anode, radially away
                  from the tank center.
          7)      voltage gradient mapping inside the tank (using cut-out coupon area).
          8)      voltage gradient mapping outside the tank as measured along the circumference
                  between every 2 anodes.
          CONTRACTOR will:
          16.     Physically Ensure complete electric isolation of coupon #1, #2 and #3 (which is closer
                  to tank shell) from the tank bottom (Do not lift the coupon).
          17.     Add sufficient weight on top of each coupon to ensure improved physical contact with
                  the soil underneath.
          18.     Try to measure coupon #1, #2 and #3 close free corrosion potential using one
                  (or more) reference electrodes placed inside the (2-in) gap between coupon and tank.
                  Do not pour any water in the gap or anywhere else.
          19.     Install one data logger per coupon to measure the coupon close and remote
                  potentials.
          20.     Measure coupon #1, #2 and #3 remote corrosion potential with the reference
                  electrode placed 20-50 m away from the tank.
          21.     Measure potential difference between coupon 1&2, 2&3, and 3&1.
          22.     Skip this step if there is no existing CP system. Turn on existing system, and measure
                  the current picked up by the coupons through the digital multimeter set to the ammeter
                  setting. Make sure to connect the leads to the proper terminals of the multimeter.
                  Turn the existing system back to off.

                  the 3-pin Megger method and confirm by measuring the resistance between the
                  coupons and the tank with the 2-pin Megger method
          24.     Re-confirm resistance measurements by using the 2-pin Megger method between
                  coupons 1 & 2, then 1 & 3, and finally 2 & 3.
